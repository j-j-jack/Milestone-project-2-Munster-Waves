Info

Garrettstown in County Cork is an exposed beach and point break that has quite reliable surf, although summer tends to be mostly flat.

Works best in offshore winds from the north. The best swell direction is from the southwest. The beach break offers both left and right hand waves. A word of warning, the surf here can be a bit dangerous at high tide and take care of submerged obstacles,groynes and rocks.

Ireland’s Wild Atlantic Way truly is a surfer’s paradise and it's unlikely to be too crowded here, even when the surf is up. When you’re successfully 'wiped out', why not head to Hurley’s for a bit of comfort food. They serve soup to steak which you can enjoy in front of a roaring fire and it tends to be a very cosy set up after a day chasing waves.
